ACTIVITIES

HERE ARE 9 ACTIVITIES YOU CAN DO IN THE CITY OF PINES:

1. Appreciate the Art:
Interested in contemporary art? Just a short drive from Baguio, the BenCab Museum in Tuba is a must-visit! Founded by renowned artist Benedicto Reyes Cabrera (BenCab), a Philippine National Artist for Visual Arts, this private museum showcases stunning works by contemporary artists.

2. Ride a boat at Burnham Park:
You can enjoy a peaceful ride on the century-old man-made lagoon while taking in the beautiful surroundings. It’s a perfect way to relax and soak up the park's charm!

3. Buy Fresh Goods at Baguio City's Public Market:
Head to the Baguio Public Market for the best pasalubong! Explore countless stalls offering local favorites like fresh strawberries, Sagada oranges, strawberry and ube jams, peanut brittle, fresh veggies, and unique souvenirs! (Check the local wares tab for more details.)

4. Buy Clothes at Ukay-Ukay's:
Second-hand stores, known locally as wagwagan or ukay-ukay to tourists, are scattered throughout downtown Baguio. You'll find endless choices of pre-loved items like clothes, bags, shoes, belts, and more.

5. Horseback Riding:
Never rode a horse before? Wright Park in Baguio offers a great selection of horses for you to try!

6. Café Hopping:
Café hopping in Baguio is a delight! Explore cozy spots with unique vibes and tasty offerings. Whether you’re after stunning views or local specialties, there’s a café for every taste in this vibrant city!

7. Try Local Street Food:
Whenever you get hungry from all of that exploring, there are plenty of food stalls and street vendors nearby. Be sure to visit the Baguio Night Market! Pop-up booths set up before dark, and evening food stalls offer a variety of tasty treats to explore.

8. Go on exciting rides at Sky Ranch Baguio:
Sky Ranch isn't exclusive to Baguio as there are similar parks in Tagaytay and Pampanga, but If you haven't tried one yet, add Sky Ranch Baguio to your list and enjoy the thrill!

9. Nature Walks:
Looking for a nature walk with friends? Check out Camp John Hay's yellow trail! This path connects Le Monet and Tree Top Adventure, leading you to Scout Hill, the final stop.
